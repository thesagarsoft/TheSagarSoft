1. WHAT INFORMATION DO WE COLLECT AND WHAT PERMISSIONS DO WE USE?

Personal information you disclose to us

In Short: We do not require you to create an account, and we do not actively collect personal identity information.

Because the HabitScreen app functions as a live wallpaper habit tracker without requiring a user account, we do not collect personal information such as your name, email address, physical address, or phone number.

Sensitive Information. We do not process sensitive information.

Information automatically collected

In Short: Some information — such as your Internet Protocol (IP) address and/or browser and device characteristics — is collected automatically when you visit our Services via third-party integrations.

We, through our third-party integrations, automatically collect certain information when you visit, use, or navigate the Services. This information does not reveal your specific identity but may include device and usage information, such as your IP address, device characteristics, operating system, language preferences, device name, country, location, information about how and when you use our Services (e.g., interaction with the app), and advertising identifiers (such as the Google Advertising ID).

Device Permissions

To provide the core functionality of the HabitScreen app, we request specific permissions on your Android device. We only use these permissions for the purposes outlined below:

  • INTERNET and ACCESS_NETWORK_STATE: These permissions allow the app to connect to the internet to serve advertisements via Google AdMob and to send anonymous crash reports and app usage data via Google Firebase Analytics.
  • POST_NOTIFICATIONS: This permission is required to send you habit-tracking reminders. When you interact with these notifications (e.g., selecting "Yes" or "No" for a habit), the app updates the live wallpaper accordingly (e.g., turning the dot green or red).
  • RECEIVE_BOOT_COMPLETED: This permission allows the app to automatically restart the live wallpaper service and reschedule your habit-tracking notifications after your device has been rebooted, ensuring you do not miss your daily habit tracking.

10. CONTROLS FOR DO-NOT-TRACK FEATURES

Most web browsers and some mobile operating systems and mobile applications include a Do-Not-Track ("DNT") feature or setting you can activate to signal your privacy preference not to have data about your online browsing activities monitored and collected. At this stage, no uniform technology standard for recognizing and implementing DNT signals has been finalized. As such, we do not currently respond to DNT browser signals or any other mechanism that automatically communicates your choice not to be tracked online.
